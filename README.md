Scoring Methodology: The 100-Point GOAT IndexThe final score is a composite index totaling 100 points, divided into four major performance pillars. Every metric is normalized against the maximum value in the dataset to ensure a fair comparison across eras.1. Career Longevity & Elite Standing (22.5%)Career Run Points (12.5 pts): Calculated as $(\frac{Player Runs}{Max Runs in Dataset}) \times 12.5$. This rewards total volume and longevity.ICC/Historical Rating (10.0 pts): Calculated as $(\frac{Peak Rating}{Max Rating in Dataset}) \times 10.0$. This captures the player’s absolute peak dominance.2. X-Factor & Match-Winning Ability (20.0%)This pillar rewards efficiency and the ability to change a game quickly.Raw X-Factor Calculation: $(Strike Rate \times 0.1) + (Century Frequency \times 5) + (MOM Awards \times 2)$Normalized Score: $(\frac{Player X\text{-}Raw}{Max X\text{-}Raw}) \times 20.0$.3. Weighted Batting Averages (WBA) (32.5%)This is the "Strength of Schedule" component. It measures performance relative to the quality of the team and the opposition.WBA Raw: $Average Relative to Team + Average Relative to Opposition$.Normalized Score: $(\frac{Player WBA}{Max WBA}) \times 32.5$.4. Consistency & Peak Impact (25.0%)Consistency Score (15.0 pts): Uses the Cons_Raw (Standard Deviation/Reliability) and Bow_Q_Raw (Quality of bowlers faced).Formula: $(\frac{Cons\_Raw + Bow\_Q\_Raw}{Max Total Consistency}) \times 15.0$.Peak Impact / IPV (10.0 pts): Uses the IPV_Raw (Impact Per Value) to measure how much a player's runs contributed to a win.Formula: $(\frac{IPV\_Raw}{Max IPV\_Raw}) \times 10.0$.
